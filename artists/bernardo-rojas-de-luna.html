Bernardo Rojas de Luna started drawing in order to explore the cosas oscuras, or dark things, inevitable to the human experience. Common themes of his work include extraterrestrials and monsters, as well as questions about love, gender, and sexuality. He sees the opportunity to represent these cosas oscuras in his work as a way to confront them and to move forward. Through this process of addressing the uncomfortable, he has developed a unique ability to approach his subject matter with a sense of familiarity and ease. Bernardo’s growing body of work reminds us that engaging with the cosas oscuras in our lives can be exciting, healing, and joyous.
