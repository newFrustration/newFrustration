Gabriela Quiñonez-Riegos is the type of artist that you don’t notice taking your picture. Which is surprising when you see her work. She seems to have mastered the art of fleshing out color in the most mundane of moments. She can bring out the glow of a room, capture the pensiveness of a stare, and the climax of a burst of laughter without drawing attention to herself.

This is a unique gift to say the least. Born into the generation that normalized photography to the point of oversaturation, Gabriela’s sense of space and her ability to document action without disturbing it sets her work apart from that of others with the same intentions.
