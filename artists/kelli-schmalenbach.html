Kelli
Schmalenbach

To watch Kelli work is to see colors converge, pushing up against each other, following the flow of the brush, which carves mountains and valleys through her paintings with the same ease as water cuts through earth. After they dry, we are left with a living record of the previous passes, highly textured shades still moving with and against each other, continuing to create a sensation of movement upon a previously stationary canvas.

It’s no surprise that she draws inspiration from the river.

“The tides are when everything comes out, everything is pushing forward, and there’s something there. There’s a new kind of life there.”

-Kelli Schmalenbach
